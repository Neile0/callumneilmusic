About

Callum Neil (b.1998) is a commercial trumpet player, arranger and composer. At a young age, Callum's love for music was birthed with inspiration from Miles Davis, Stevie Wonder, Snarky Puppy and more. Whilst Callum was at secondary school, he played with community bands such as the Byres Road Big Band and Zig Zag Big Band, and went on to perform and record with the National Youth Jazz Orchestra of Scotland w/ Iain Ballamy. By the time Callum was 15, he had began composing and arranging for The Cosmonaut Arkestra, releasing their EP "Crash Landing". At 17 Callum began studying at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland, studying BMus (Jazz) under the tutelage of Tom MacNiven (Hue & Cry, SNJO). Callum has performed at various events and stages such as the EFG London Jazz Festival and performances for the 2014 Glasgow Commonwealth Games. As an educator Callum has been a tutor on the Scottish Chamber Orchestra’s “VIBE” project with Paul Griffiths, Callum has participated in workshopping with Drake Music Scotland —a creative charity for musicians with disabilities and additional support needs— including workshop led by Snarky Puppy’s Michael League.
