Callum Neil is a trumpet player, arranger/composer and educator. Callum's musical journey started at a very young age as he was nurtured by music both at home and within some of Glasgow’s community bands. This would lead him to perform and record with the National Youth Jazz Orchestra of Scotland with soloist Iain Ballamy under the direction of Malcolm Edmonstone and Andrew Bain.

Callum had performed at various events and venues such as the EFG London Jazz Festival, Glasgow Jazz Festival and performances for the 2014 Glasgow Commonwealth Games.

At the age of 17, Callum left secondary school to study at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland. Here, he studied for a Bachelor of Music (Jazz) a course lead by Tommy Smith. As part of his this he was given the opportunity to play with world class musicians such as Laura Jurd, Neil Yates and Mike Stern.
As an educator Callum has worked with the Scottish Chamber Orchestra where he has been a part of projects such as "VIBE" lead by Paul Griffiths and “Instrument Armoury” enabling young people to gain a taster for music and brass playing.

Callum has also participated in workshops and courses with Drake Music Scotland — a creative charity for musicians with disabilities and additional support needs — One of which was was lead by Micheal League (Snarky Puppy) and has a eagerness to pursue projects of this nature.

Callum’s musical influences include: Miles Davis, Stevie Wonder, Kenny Wheeler, Mike Stern, Snarky Puppy, Janek Gwizdala, Bob Reynolds and many more.
